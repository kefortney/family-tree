The Anderson branch is the paternal line of Jill Andersen Fortney — the line that carries the Andersen surname. Andrew B. Andersen II married Shirley Mulhearn (daughter of Robert and Agnes Mulhearn), connecting the Andersen and Mulhearn lines and eventually, through their daughter Jill, converging with the Fortney and Södergren lines.

The pre-Andrew genealogy for this branch has not yet been documented. This page serves as a placeholder for research contributions from family members who may have additional records, memories, or documents.

Andrew B. Andersen II & Shirley Mulhearn

Jill Andersen Fortney

Jill Andersen Fortney married Alan Jon Fortney, bringing together all four family branches represented on this site. She is an accomplished photographer — many of the beautiful Norway photographs in the gallery, taken during the 2005 visit to the ancestral Forthun farm, are credited to her.

Together with Alan, Jill met with Torill Susanne Magnusdotter Yttri Otterhjell, the current owner of the Forthun farm (Bnr #8), and helped verify and expand the genealogical record of the Norwegian branch.

The Andersen–Mulhearn Connection

What makes this branch particularly interesting is that it serves as the direct bridge between the Mulhearn/O'Brien Irish Catholic line and the Fortney Norwegian Lutheran line. Andrew B. Andersen II married into the Mulhearn family, and his daughter Jill married into the Fortney family — connecting four very different cultural and religious traditions into one American family story.

The Andersen surname itself suggests Scandinavian roots — possibly Norwegian or Danish (Anderson/Andersen being among the most common names in those countries). If so, there may be a pleasing symmetry: both the Fortney line and the Andersen line could trace back to Scandinavia, meeting again in Wisconsin after centuries apart.
