The Four Branches

Each family line carries a distinct heritage across centuries and continents.

⚓

Fortney / Forthun

Norwegian Origins · 1570 – Present

From the Forthun farm in Luster, Norway — a mountain parcel worked for over 450 years — to the Wisconsin frontier. The main line descends from Aamund Jonson Forthun (b. 1570) through seven generations to Hans Thomassen Fortney, who moved the family to Wheeler, Wisconsin in 1907.

Södergren

Swedish-American Lutheran · Moline, Illinois

Rev. Carl Johannes Södergren and Agatha Elizabeth Chester raised a family steeped in Lutheran ministry. Their daughter Anita Linnea Södergren (1907–1990) married Chaplain Major Albin Leonard Fortney in 1935 and spent her life in 25 homes across the country and Europe.

Mulhearn / O'Brien

Irish Origins · County Clare & Donegal

William Mulhearn emigrated from Ballyshannon, County Clare; the McEntee line comes from County Donegal (Mac an tSaoi, "Son of the Scholar"); and the O'Briens trace descent from Brian Boru himself. These three lines converge in Newark, New Jersey and eventually in Wisconsin.

Read the Mulhearn story →
★

Andersen / Andersen

American · Wisconsin

Andrew B. Andersen II married Shirley Mulhearn, connecting the Mulhearn and Andersen lines. Their daughter Jill Andersen married Alan Jon Fortney, bringing all four branches together in the next generation.
