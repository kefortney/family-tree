The Södergren family was deeply rooted in Lutheran ministry. Rev. Carl Johannes Södergren and his wife Agatha Elizabeth Chester raised six children — multiple sons became ministers, and the family's faith shaped the course of the Fortney line for generations.

Anita Linnea Södergren was born on 17 February 1907 in Moline, Illinois; the family soon moved to Burlington, Iowa. She was the fifth of six known children.

Rev. Carl Johannes Södergren's Children

  1. Una Elizabeth Södergren — married John Milton, MD
  2. Rev. Carl Wendell Södergren — Lutheran minister, married Eunice. During WWII, Anita's family stayed in Portland, Oregon, near Carl Wendell and his family.

Anita Linnea Södergren

Anita Linnea Södergren (b. 17 February 1907, Moline, IL; d. 4 July 1990, Stoughton, WI) married Albin Leonard Fortney on 28 May 1935 in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

Anita was a practical, quietly fierce woman. When Albin received a garbled cable during the war advising her to "keep the appointment" — she was baffled, then relieved when the correction came: "keep the apartment." When their son Steven developed a deep Southern accent from the family's Black nanny in Charleston, she found it funny. When she received word that Albin was in Hawaii as Pearl Harbor was being bombed — with her family booked to arrive on December 24 — she managed the crisis with her characteristic composure, relocating the family to Portland to be near her brother Carl.

"Anita often remarked that she had four children in four different cities and four different states — and indicated she didn't need more."

The family's life was defined by constant movement — 25 homes in the course of their marriage. From filling stations in Minnesota to Europe in 1947, from rural Wisconsin parishes to overseas postings in Germany and Austria. Through it all, Anita held the household together.

Her Children

  • Steven Dale Fortney (b. 2 January 1937, Minneapolis) — Author & professor. Has four children.
  • Alan Jon Fortney (b. 18 November 1939, Rocky River, OH) — Married Jill Andersen. Primary compiler of the family history.
  • Kendall Thomas Fortney (b. 2 June 1941, Niagara Falls, NY; KIA 6 February 1968, Vietnam) — Army medic. Named for his uncle Thomas Kendahl. Initially listed as MIA; identified with the help of Senator William Proxmire. Buried Stoughton, WI, March 1968.
  • Anita Linnea Fortney (b. 3 July 1947, Tacoma, WA) — Married William Harms; moved to Muskegon, MI.

The Last Years

After Albin died on 18 May 1968, Anita remained in the family home on North Street in Stoughton — their 25th and final home — for over two decades. She eventually moved to Skaalen Home, a Lutheran care facility in Stoughton.

Anita Linnea Södergren Fortney died on 4 July 1990 — before breakfast — after 22 years, 2 months, and 6 days as a widow. She had lived 83 years, 4 months, and 15 days.

The Piepkorn Connection

One of Anita's sisters married Rev. Arthur Carl Piepkorn — a remarkably decorated figure: PhD, ThD, and Colonel in the U.S. Army. When Albin was discharged from the Army at Carlisle Barracks in Pennsylvania (after his heart episode in Vienna), Arthur and "Aunt Miriam" Piepkorn were stationed there. The Fortney boys — Steven, Alan, and Kendall — caught fireflies with the Piepkorn cousins Mary, Faith, Felicity, and Angela in the summer evenings.
